iLovePDF: Detailed Review

iLovePDF is one of the most popular online services for working with PDFs. It offers a wide range of tools: compression, merging, splitting, conversion, and more.

iLovePDF Pros

  • Large set of tools (over 25)
  • Desktop application available
  • Batch processing support in paid version
  • Cloud storage integration

iLovePDF Cons

  • Files are uploaded to a server — privacy risk
  • Free version limited to 1-2 operations per hour
  • Ads in the free version
  • Full functionality requires a subscription from $7/mo

SmallPDF: Detailed Review

SmallPDF positions itself as an "all-in-one" solution for working with PDFs. The service offers a pleasant interface and many tools.

SmallPDF Pros

  • Pleasant and intuitive interface
  • Electronic document signing
  • Google Drive and Dropbox integration
  • Mobile application

SmallPDF Cons

  • Files are uploaded to a server
  • Very strict free version limits — 2 operations per day
  • Most expensive subscription among competitors — from $12/mo
  • Many features require a paid plan

PDF Tools: Detailed Review

PDF Tools is a next-generation service that processes files directly in the user's browser, without uploading to a server.

PDF Tools Pros

  • Completely free — all features available without payment
  • Files never leave your computer — maximum privacy
  • No limits on the number of operations
  • No registration required
  • No ads
  • Works offline after loading the page

PDF Tools Cons

  • Fewer tools than competitors (but all essential ones are included)
  • No desktop application (works in the browser)
  • No cloud storage integration

The Key Difference: Privacy

Both iLovePDF and SmallPDF upload your files to their servers for processing. This means your confidential documents pass through someone else's infrastructure. Even if the services promise to delete files, you have no way to verify this.

PDF Tools processes files exclusively in your browser. Files are never sent anywhere. You can verify this by disconnecting from the internet — all tools will continue to work.

This is a key advantage for those who work with confidential documents: lawyers, accountants, doctors, and corporate users.

Cost: Who Wins?

If you regularly work with PDFs and use iLovePDF or SmallPDF, you will quickly hit the limits of the free version. An iLovePDF subscription costs from $7/mo ($84/year), SmallPDF from $12/mo ($144/year).

PDF Tools is completely free, with no limits. For students, freelancers, and small businesses, that's a savings of $84-144 per year.
